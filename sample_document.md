# Aurora Coffee Machine — User Manual (Sample Document)

Welcome to the Aurora X200 automatic espresso machine. This manual covers setup, daily use, maintenance, troubleshooting, and warranty terms.

## 1. Getting Started

Unbox the machine and remove all protective film. Place the Aurora X200 on a flat, dry surface at least 10 cm away from walls to allow ventilation. Fill the water tank (1.8 L capacity) with cold, filtered water only. Do not use distilled or sparkling water, as this can damage the internal sensors.

Before first use, run two cleaning cycles: press and hold the RINSE button for 3 seconds. The machine will flush hot water through the brewing unit. Discard this water.

## 2. Brewing Your First Coffee

Fill the bean hopper with whole roasted beans (maximum 250 g). The Aurora X200 supports five grind settings, adjustable with the dial inside the hopper. Setting 1 is the finest (best for espresso) and setting 5 the coarsest (best for lungo).

To brew: place a cup under the spout, select your drink on the touch panel (Espresso, Americano, Cappuccino, Latte, or Flat White), and press START. The default espresso shot is 40 ml at 92°C. You can customize volume (20–120 ml) and temperature (88–96°C) in the Settings menu.

## 3. Milk System

The Aurora X200 includes an automatic milk frother with a detachable carafe (0.6 L). For best foam, use cold whole milk. Oat and soy milk are supported via the PLANT MILK mode, which lowers the frothing temperature to 60°C to prevent curdling.

Clean the milk system after every use by pressing the AUTO-CLEAN button. The carafe is dishwasher safe (top rack only).

## 4. Maintenance Schedule

- Daily: empty the drip tray and grounds container; run AUTO-CLEAN on the milk system.
- Weekly: rinse the water tank and wipe the housing with a damp cloth.
- Monthly: remove and rinse the brewing unit under warm water. Do not use soap on the brewing unit.
- Every 3 months (or when the DESCALE light turns on): run a full descaling cycle using Aurora descaling solution. The descaling cycle takes approximately 25 minutes.

Failure to descale when indicated may void the warranty for heating-element failures.

## 5. Troubleshooting

**Machine won't turn on:** Check that the power cord is fully inserted and the rear master switch is set to I. If the display stays dark, hold the power button for 10 seconds to reset.

**Coffee is too weak:** Use a finer grind setting (turn the dial toward 1), increase the dose in Settings > Strength, or check that the bean hopper is not empty.

**Error E05:** The brewing unit is not seated correctly. Open the service door, remove the brewing unit, rinse it, and re-insert until it clicks.

**Error E12:** Water flow blocked — usually limescale. Run a descaling cycle. If the error persists after descaling, contact support.

**Milk won't froth:** Ensure the frother intake tube is fully attached and not clogged. Run AUTO-CLEAN twice. Use cold milk; warm milk does not foam well.

## 6. Warranty and Support

The Aurora X200 is covered by a 24-month limited warranty from the date of purchase, covering manufacturing defects. The warranty does not cover damage caused by limescale buildup (missed descaling), use of non-approved cleaning agents, or commercial use exceeding 30 drinks per day.

For support, contact support@aurora-coffee.example or call the hotline listed on the warranty card. Keep your proof of purchase; it is required for all warranty claims.
